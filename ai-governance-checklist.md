# AI Governance Checklist

This checklist helps organizations assess whether an AI use case is ready for responsible, secure, and enterprise-ready adoption.

AI governance is not only about compliance. It is about making sure AI systems are useful, safe, explainable, monitored, and accountable.

---

## 1. Business Purpose

Before starting any AI use case, clarify the business reason.

Questions:

- What business problem does this AI system solve?
- Who is the target user?
- What decision or task will the AI support?
- What measurable value should it create?
- Is AI the right solution for this problem?

---

## 2. Data Assessment

AI systems depend on data quality, sensitivity, and access control.

Questions:

- What data will the AI system use?
- Is the data internal, external, public, confidential, or regulated?
- Does the system use personal data?
- Is sensitive business data involved?
- Are data access permissions clearly defined?
- Is the data accurate, current, and relevant?

---

## 3. Risk Classification

Not all AI use cases carry the same level of risk.

Classify the use case as:

- Low risk
- Medium risk
- High risk

High-risk use cases may include:

- Legal decisions
- Financial recommendations
- HR decisions
- Compliance outputs
- Customer-impacting decisions
- Safety-critical workflows

---

## 4. Human Review

Human review should be added when AI output can create material risk.

Questions:

- Who reviews the AI output?
- Is expert approval required?
- Can the user edit or reject the output?
- Is there an escalation path?
- Does the system prevent fully autonomous high-risk actions?

---

## 5. Model and Output Quality

AI output should be evaluated before production use.

Questions:

- How accurate is the output?
- Has the output been tested with real examples?
- Does the system handle uncertainty?
- Does it avoid unsupported claims?
- Does it cite sources where required?
- How will wrong outputs be detected?

---

## 6. Security and Privacy

AI systems must protect users, documents, and enterprise data.

Questions:

- Is access controlled?
- Are documents stored securely?
- Are prompts and outputs logged safely?
- Are secrets, API keys, and credentials protected?
- Is sensitive data masked where needed?
- Are retention policies defined?

---

## 7. Monitoring and Auditability

AI systems should be monitored after deployment.

Questions:

- Are prompts and outputs logged?
- Can decisions be audited?
- Are user feedback and corrections captured?
- Are failure cases reviewed?
- Are performance and risk metrics tracked?
- Is there a process for continuous improvement?

---

## 8. Ownership and Accountability

Every AI system needs clear ownership.

Questions:

- Who owns the AI use case?
- Who owns the data?
- Who owns the model risk?
- Who approves deployment?
- Who handles incidents?
- Who monitors business impact?

---

## Deployment Readiness Summary

An AI use case should move to production only when:

- Business value is clear
- Data risks are understood
- Human review is defined
- Security controls are in place
- Output quality has been tested
- Monitoring is available
- Ownership is assigned
- Governance controls are documented

---

## Conclusion

Good AI governance helps organizations adopt AI faster with confidence.

The goal is not to block AI adoption. The goal is to make AI adoption safer, more reliable, and more valuable.
